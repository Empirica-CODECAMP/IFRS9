IFRS 9 – Background

Post 2008 financial crisis, regulators around the world realized that history-based accounting standards can be extremely misleading and a paradigm shift in accounting methodologies is needed.

In July 2014, IASB issued IFRS 9 standard which replaced existing IAS 39 provisioning method. The IASB stated that IAS 39 delayed recognition of credit losses; a forward-looking approach is introduced.

IFRS 9 became effective globally in Jan 2018 and posed huge challenges for accounting firms and institutions unfamiliar with predictive analytics or expected loss modeling.
